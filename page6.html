5. POWER

Power, in relation to authority, refers to the ability of an individual or a group to influence others, enforce decisions, and control resources or policies within a specific context or organization.
Authority typically denotes the legitimate right to exercise power, often derived from a formal position, expertise, or societal norms.
Authority gives individuals or institutions the right to make decisions, issue commands, and expect compliance from others.
It is essential in maintaining order, governance, and organizational structure within societies and institutions, ensuring that decisions are made effectively and responsibilities are fulfilled.

Quotes

Here are five notable quotes about power:
1. "Power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely." - Lord Acton
2. "Nearly all men can stand adversity, but if you want to test a man's character, give him power." - Abraham Lincoln
3. "Knowledge is power." - Francis Bacon
4. "The measure of a man is what he does with power." - Plato
5. "The most common way people give up their power is by thinking they don't have any." - Alice Walker

Note:These quotes reflect various perspectives on power, highlighting its potential for corruption, its relationship with character and responsibility, and the importance of recognizing and using one's own power effectively.
